How the sound mapping works
Pitch mapping: Each bar (1 to N) maps linearly. freq = 120 + (value/total) * 1200

Oscillators: Sine = pure. Triangle = warm harmonics. Sawtooth = bright, all harmonics. Square = hollow/retro. FM = carrier modulated at 2x for bell tones.

SoundFont mode: Bar values map to MIDI 36-96 (C2-C7). The SF2 parser reads RIFF/sfbk structure, locates instrument headers (inst), instrument bags (ibag), generators (igen, oper 53 = sampleID), and sample headers (shdr) to extract per-instrument PCM data. Each instrument gets its own audio buffer with the correct sample rate and original pitch for accurate playback rate shifting.

Multi-instrument SF2: The parser walks the inst chunk to find all instruments, follows the ibag/igen chain to resolve which sample each instrument references, then extracts that sample region from the smpl chunk. The instrument selector lets you switch between them live. Falls back to raw sample enumeration if the inst chain is missing or malformed.

Why it sounds musical: As sorting progresses, nearby bars converge to similar values, producing melodic runs. The completion sweep plays every bar in order for a clean ascending scale.
